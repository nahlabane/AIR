What is AIR Mentorship Network?

The AIR Mentorship Network is the first active programme of the African Industrial Revolution (AIR). It is a volunteer-driven educational initiative established to connect learners with mentors who are willing to share their knowledge, experience and skills to help build a stronger future for Africa.

AIR believes that education should not be limited by geography, financial circumstances or access to opportunities. Through mentorship, academic support and collaboration, the programme creates opportunities for learners to receive guidance from university students, graduates, professionals, entrepreneurs and experienced individuals who volunteer their time to support the next generation.

The programme operates primarily through online platforms, allowing mentors and learners to connect regardless of their physical location. This approach enables knowledge sharing across communities while reducing geographical barriers that often limit access to quality mentorship.

AIR Mentorship Network is built on the belief that Africans have the knowledge, talent and willingness to uplift one another. Every mentor who joins the programme contributes to a growing network of people committed to helping learners achieve their educational and career goals.

Although AIR serves as the coordinating and administrative platform for the programme, the AIR Mentorship Network belongs to the people who build it. Its success depends on the collective efforts of volunteer mentors, learners, educators, professionals, organisations and communities working together to improve education across Africa.

The long-term vision is to establish a sustainable mentorship network that supports learners across African countries while respecting each country's educational system and curriculum. Expansion will take place as dedicated volunteer mentors and local support networks are established within each country.

How the AIR Mentorship Network Operates

The AIR Mentorship Network is built on collaboration, volunteerism and the belief that knowledge becomes more valuable when it is shared. The programme brings together learners and volunteer mentors through online platforms, making academic support more accessible regardless of location.

Learners begin by registering through the AIR website. Their registration allows AIR to understand which grades, subjects and countries require support. This information also helps identify where additional volunteer mentors are needed before new learning groups are introduced.

Individuals who wish to become mentors register as volunteers through the AIR website. Mentors include university students, graduates, professionals, entrepreneurs and other experienced individuals who are willing to share their knowledge and support learners in their academic journey.

At present, learners and mentors communicate through a shared WhatsApp Community. This serves as the central communication platform where announcements, learning resources, study material, mentorship opportunities and important programme updates are shared.

As the AIR Mentorship Network continues to grow, dedicated communities and groups will be introduced to organise learners and mentors according to their roles, grades and subjects. This will allow more focused communication and improve the learning experience for everyone involved.

Volunteer mentors support learners through academic guidance, answering questions, explaining concepts, sharing study material, recording educational videos and hosting online learning sessions. Live lessons may be conducted using platforms such as Microsoft Teams or Google Meet, allowing learners to interact directly with mentors and ask questions in real time.

Whenever possible, live sessions will be recorded and made available through the AIR learning platform so that learners who could not attend or who wish to revise the lesson can watch it at a later stage.

Every mentor contributes according to their availability, expertise and commitment. AIR values sustainable volunteerism, recognising that many mentors are students or working professionals who generously dedicate their personal time and resources to support African learners.

The programme continues to evolve as new volunteers join, new subjects become active and additional learning opportunities are introduced. Every learner registration and every new volunteer mentor strengthens the network and helps expand educational opportunities across Africa.

Current Programme Focus

The AIR Mentorship Network is currently providing academic support to Grade 12 learners in Mathematics and Physical Sciences. These subjects have been activated because AIR currently has dedicated volunteer mentors who are committed to supporting learners consistently.

AIR welcomes learner registrations even for grades, subjects and countries that are not yet active. Every registration helps AIR understand where support is needed most while volunteer mentors are being recruited.

The programme is also open to recruiting volunteer mentors from every African country. AIR does not limit mentor recruitment to South Africa. As soon as a country has enough dedicated volunteer mentors to provide reliable academic support, AIR will begin accepting learner registrations from that country and officially launch the programme there.

This approach allows every participating country to build a local mentorship team that understands its own education system, curriculum and learning requirements. It also creates a more meaningful learning experience because learners are supported by mentors who are familiar with their country's syllabus.

The same principle applies to grades and subjects. AIR currently supports Grade 12 Mathematics and Physical Sciences because these are the areas where dedicated volunteer mentors are available.

As more volunteers join the programme, AIR will gradually activate support for additional grades, including Grade 10 and 11. Additional school subjects will also be introduced as qualified volunteer mentors become available.

Learners are therefore encouraged to register even if their grade or subject has not yet been activated. Once AIR has recruited enough dedicated mentors for that grade or subject, registered learners will be invited to participate as the programme expands.

At present, learners and mentors communicate through a shared WhatsApp Community. As the AIR Mentorship Network continues to grow, this structure will gradually evolve into dedicated communities and groups organised according to roles, grades and subjects.

Future learning communities may include separate groups for Mathematics Learners,Physical Sciences Learners and additional subject groups as new volunteer mentors join the programme.

AIR believes in sustainable growth. A new country, subject or grade is only activated when there are enough committed volunteer mentors to provide meaningful and consistent academic support to learners.

Building the Network Together

The AIR Mentorship Network is built on the belief that Africa's greatest resource is its people. Every learner, mentor, volunteer, educator, organisation and community has something valuable to contribute towards improving education across the continent.

AIR does not view education as the responsibility of schools alone. We believe that communities, universities, professionals, entrepreneurs and graduates all have an important role to play in developing the next generation by sharing their knowledge, experience and skills.

Our volunteer mentors are the foundation of this programme. Many of them are university students or working professionals who generously volunteer their own time to mentor learners, answer academic questions, prepare learning resources and record educational videos that benefit many learners.

In many cases, our mentors use their own laptops, mobile devices and internet data to participate in the programme. Their commitment demonstrates that meaningful change does not always begin with large financial resources—it often begins with people who are willing to help.

Many learners also face challenges beyond the classroom. Access to affordable internet, smartphones, tablets and laptops remains a barrier for some young people who are eager to learn and participate in online educational activities.

This is why AIR welcomes individuals, organisations, businesses and communities that wish to support the programme. Every contribution, whether it is knowledge, time, equipment, internet data or financial support, helps remove barriers to education and creates opportunities for more learners to succeed.

The growth of the AIR Mentorship Network depends on people who believe that education can transform communities. Every new volunteer mentor allows another subject, another grade or even another country to become active within the programme.

AIR serves as the coordinating and administrative platform, but the programme belongs to everyone who contributes towards its success. Together, we are building a network that empowers learners, strengthens communities and invests in Africa's future.

Partner with AIR

Partnerships may include sponsorships, collaborative projects, educational programmes or resource sharing.

Introduce AIR to Your Community

One of the simplest ways to support AIR is by introducing the programme to your school, university, workplace or local community. Every introduction creates opportunities for new learners, volunteer mentors and partners to become involved.

Every contribution helps AIR move closer to its vision of making quality mentorship and educational support accessible to learners across Africa.

If you have a project, initiative or idea that can make a positive difference in your community, we invite you to share it with us. The AIR Mentorship Network belongs to Africa. AIR serves as the coordinating and administrative platform, but its growth depends on Africans who are willing to contribute their knowledge, experience, creativity and leadership to build something that benefits present and future generations.

Every line of code written, every educational resource created, every lesson recorded, every idea shared and every hour volunteered becomes an investment in Africa's future.

Future Learning Platform

The AIR Mentorship Network is continuously evolving to provide a more organised, accessible and engaging learning experience for learners, mentors, volunteers and administrators across Africa. As the programme grows, AIR plans to introduce a dedicated online learning platform that will bring together educational resources, mentorship and collaboration in one place.

The future platform is intended to support the daily activities of the AIR Mentorship Network while making it easier for learners to access educational content and for volunteer mentors to share knowledge with communities across Africa.

Learner Portal

Registered learners will have access to a personal learning portal where they can view announcements, access study material, watch recorded lessons, attend live learning sessions, download educational resources and communicate with mentors.

Mentor Portal

Volunteer mentors will have a dedicated workspace to upload study material, record educational videos, schedule learning sessions, communicate with learners and collaborate with other mentors to improve the quality of educational support.

Administrator Portal

AIR administrators will manage learner registrations, mentor applications, educational content, announcements, volunteer coordination and programme activities through a secure administrative platform.

Live Learning Sessions

AIR plans to organise scheduled live learning sessions using platforms such as Microsoft Teams and Google Meet. These sessions will allow learners to interact directly with volunteer mentors, ask questions and participate in collaborative learning.

Recorded Learning Library

Whenever possible, live sessions will be recorded and organised into a growing digital learning library. This will allow learners to revisit previous lessons, revise difficult topics and continue learning at their own pace.

Learning Resources

The platform will continue expanding its collection of study guides, revision notes, examination preparation material, practice activities, educational videos and other learning resources contributed by volunteer mentors and educational partners.

Growing Together

The future AIR Learning Platform will continue to grow as the network expands into additional countries, grades and subjects. Every new volunteer mentor, learner, partner and contributor helps shape the future of this platform and strengthens its ability to serve communities across Africa.
